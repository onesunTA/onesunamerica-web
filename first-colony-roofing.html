First Colony is a master-planned community in Sugar Land governed by the First Colony Community Association (FCA). All roofing projects require FCA pre-approval before work begins — homeowners must submit materials, colors, and contractor details for review. Skipping this step can result in fines and mandatory removal of unapproved work. One Sun America manages the FCA approval process on behalf of our clients: we prepare the required submittal documents, coordinate the approval timeline, and begin your project only once the HOA has confirmed in writing.

The age of First Colony homes makes this community one of the most active roofing markets in the Houston suburbs. Homes built in the 1980s and 1990s are well past the design life of their original roofing systems, and even homes from the late 1990s and early 2000s are approaching 25+ years — the point where most architectural shingle systems begin showing significant deterioration. Fort Bend County, including First Colony, sits in the southern extent of Houston's primary hail corridor, receiving 4–6 significant hail events annually, which accelerates roof wear further.
