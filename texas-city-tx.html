About Texas City, TX

Gulf Coast Roofing for
Texas City Homeowners

GULF COAST STORM ZONE — CLAIM DEADLINES ARE STRICT
Texas City is in the Gulf Coast storm zone. After every named storm, we offer rapid inspection scheduling. Texas law gives you one year from the date of loss to file a claim — don't wait.

Texas City sits on Galveston Bay, putting it on the front line of Gulf Coast storm systems. With a population of approximately 50,000 across the city and La Marque, and home values ranging from $160,000 to $270,000, this is a working waterfront community with real exposure to the full range of Texas coastal weather — named hurricanes, tropical storms, and 4–5 hail events per year.

Hurricane Ike (2008) caused catastrophic roof and structural damage throughout Texas City and La Marque. Many homes were rebuilt or extensively repaired — but those repairs are now 16+ years old and have been further stressed by Harvey (2017) and Hurricane Beryl (2024), which brought significant wind damage back to the area. Coastal homes repaired after Ike may be approaching their next replacement cycle, and any Beryl damage represents a current insurance claim opportunity.

Texas City's coastal proximity also means salt-air corrosion is a real factor. Standard asphalt shingles degrade faster in salt-air environments — granule loss accelerates, and flashing and fasteners corrode more quickly than in inland areas. This is one reason metal roofing is increasingly popular here: standing seam metal resists corrosion, handles 140+ mph winds, and can last 40–70 years with minimal maintenance.

We serve neighborhoods throughout Texas City including the Bay Street area, Lago Mar, the Texas City Prairie, La Marque adjacent communities, and the broader Mainland area of Galveston County. Whether your damage is from the latest named storm or aging Ike-era repairs, One Sun America will give you an honest, documented assessment.

Daño por Viento Huracán Beryl (2024) — Reemplazo Total, Seguro Completamente Procesado

A Texas City homeowner in the Lago Mar area contacted us two weeks after Hurricane Beryl made landfall. Despite the roof being only 8 years old, Beryl's sustained winds had lifted and cracked multiple shingle sections and compromised the ridge cap across the entire length of the roofline. Our inspection documented the damage with GPS-timestamped photos tied to the storm event. We worked with a Public Adjuster to submit the claim, and the insurance company approved full replacement with CertainTeed Landmark IR shingles — 26 squares — with the homeowner only responsible for their deductible. The project was completed in two days, including full perimeter cleanup.
